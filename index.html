You’ve already taken a positive step by investing in the Meezan Tahaffuz Pension Fund (MTPF) this tax year.

However, your current contribution may still be below your eligible tax-credit limit. Under Section 63 of the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001, eligible investors may claim tax credit on pension fund investments of up to 20% of taxable income. So, if you still have room left, adding more to your MTPF account before the tax year closes could help strengthen your retirement savings while improving your tax planning.

Your Tax Credit — By Profile

Section 63, Income Tax Ordinance 2001 · Up to 20% of taxable income

Salaried Individual

Invest up to 20% of your taxable income in MTPF and reduce your monthly income tax — automatically.

How to claim: Submit your MTPF account statement to your HR or Finance Department. Your tax is adjusted directly from monthly payroll — no filing needed.

Self-Employed

Invest up to 20% of your taxable income in MTPF and reduce your payable tax.

How to claim: Declare your MTPF investment in your Wealth Statement when filing your annual income tax return. The credit is applied against your total tax liability.
